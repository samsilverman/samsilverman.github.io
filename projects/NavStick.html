NavStick: Self-Directed Navigation for People with Visual Impairments



Audio navigation tools seek to help visually impaired people (VIPs) navigate independently. Today’s tools act as guides that users must follow to reach destinations. Navigating independently is about more than simply being guided, however, independent navigation is also about the ability to look around, to form a cognitive map of one’s environments.

To this end, we introduce NavStick, an audio navigation tool for looking around and navigating in a self-directed way. NavStick repurposes a game controller’s thumbstick to allow VIPs to survey what is around them via line-of-sight.

Research Project in the Computer-Enabled Abilities Laboratory (CEAL)
