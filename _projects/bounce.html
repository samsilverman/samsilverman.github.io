Bounce! allows players to work together in keeping a ball rolling on a board controlled by physically-modeled ropes.

System Description

Bounce! is built in Unity, and makes use of the Mercury Messaging Framework developed by CGUI. Users use a head-warn display to and associated controllers to play Bounce!
Each handle is gripped and controlled by a single Touch controller, allowing participants to manipulate the handles with six degrees of freedom. As the handles are moved in space, they pull on the ropes, which in turn move the board. -- Elvezio, et al.

My Work

I am currently working in the CGUI Lab researching ways to improve Bounce! in the following ways:
  1. Develop a way to test and play Bounce! as a single player. This involves building a bot to reasonably interact with the player in a test and play setting.
  2. Expand the gameplay beyond a featureless planar platform containing a single ball.
Additionally, but not directly related to Bounce!, I am investigating ways to expand Mercury Messaging Framework's networking support beyond Unity's deprecated UNet Networking API. This will allow Bounce! and other networked projects relying on Mercury to continue functioning properly into the future.

Significance of Research

Bounce! has many contributions and applications to VR. Some highlighted by the CGUI team include:
  1. Advancement of low latency collaborative virtual environments.
  2. Example application: Motor rehabilitation, in which therapists and patients can work together.
