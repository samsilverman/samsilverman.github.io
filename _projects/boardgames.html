Specifically, I helped investigate the ways to incorporate AR with board and card games to provide an individual the feeling of being in person and playing together when remote.

My Work

As one of the first research projects in CEAL, I helped to start this new project by:
  1. Performing technology reviews on existing platforms in the remote board and card games space such as Tabletop Simulator.
  2. Formulating initial research questions for the project.

    The initial research questions revolve around the main theme of what level of presence is needed to give players an experience that is as rich as if they were in person? This question is multi-dimensional as it encompasses many aspects of games, all of which can be represented in different levels of fidelity using AR:

    Dimensions of fidelity in AR board games

  3. Designing and administering the first round of user studies to test the research question: To what extent is a physical representation of players necessary?

    My responsibilities touched on all aspects of the user study design including preparing experimental design (target user population, hypothesis, variables/confounds, etc.), recruitment material, surveys, and writing the institutional review board (IRB) protocol.

Significance of Research

  1. By identifying important characteristics in remote communication in a game setting, this project will serve as a guide for current and future remote game technologies to significantly improve the user experience by increasing ones feeling of co-presence. The importance of feeling together while remote is something significantly valued by society since the COVID-19 pandemic.
  2. Findings from this project may help improve remote tasks in other domains. Two examples are meetings and teaching.
