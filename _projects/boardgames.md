I worked in the Computer-Enabled Abilities Laboratory (CEAL), researching ways in which Augmented Reality (AR) can be used as a social tool to provide a feeling of co-presence. Specifically, I helped investigate the ways to incorporate AR with board and card games to provide an individual the feeling of being in person and playing together when remote.

## My Work

As one of the first research projects in CEAL, I helped to start this new project by:

1. Performing technology reviews on existing platforms in the remote board and card games space such as [Tabletop Simulator](https://store.steampowered.com/app/286160/Tabletop_Simulator/){:target="_blank"}.

2. Formulating initial research questions for the project.

    The initial research questions revolve around the main theme of *what level of presence is needed to give players an experience that is as rich as if they were in person?* This question is multi-dimensional as it encompasses many aspects of games, all of which can be represented in different levels of fidelity using AR:

    FIGURE (11/20/19)

3. Designing and administering the first round of user studies to test the research question: *To what extent is a physical representation of players nesessary?*

    My responsibilities touched on all aspects of the user study design including preparing experimental design (target user population, hypothesis, variables/confounds, etc.), recruitment material, surveys, and writing the institutional review board (IRB) protocol.

## Significance of Research

1. By identifying important characteristics in remote communcation in a game setting, this project will serve as a guide for current and future remote game technologies to significantly improve the user expereince by increasing ones feeling of co-presence. The importance of feeling together while remote is something significally valued by society since the COVID-19 pandemic.

2. Findings from this project may help improve remote tasks in other domains. Two examples are be meetings, teaching.
