--- permalink: / title: "About Me" excerpt: "About me" author_profile: true redirect_from: - /about/ - /about.html - /home/ - /home.html --- I am a master’s student at Columbia University studying Computer Science with a concentration in Vision, Graphics, Interaction, and Robotics. My research interests include artificial intelligence, human-computer interaction, robotics, augmented and virtual reality, and computational fabrication.

I aim to advance the intelligence of physical computing systems to simultaneously improve these systems' capabilities and enhance their usability.

As a research assistant in Prof. Brian A. Smith's Computer-Enabled Abilities Laboratory (CEAL) and Prof. Steven K. Feiner's Computer Graphics and User Interfaces (CGUI) Laboratory, I have worked on research projects in accessibility and virtual reality. These projects share the common goal of maximizing users' experience by enhancing interaction with an intelligent system.

I received my bachelor's degree in Computer Science from the University of Massachusetts Amherst. In previous summers I have interned at The MathWorks and Rocket Software working on software engineering projects.
